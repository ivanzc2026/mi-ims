Quest MI — Automation Roadmap

Three-horizon roadmap from fall quick wins through Chipp Bot deployment and long-term platform architecture.
Horizon 1 = build for fall 2026. Horizon 2 = SY26-27 Q1 (mid-term). Horizon 3 = long-term platform decisions.

Horizon 1 — Fall 2026
Quick wins with existing tools
Airtable + Zapier + Google Forms + Reach 360 + Calendly. No new platforms. ~20–25 hours total build time. Deployable before fall launch.
Horizon 2 — SY26-27 Q1
Chipp Bot + mid-term improvements
Teacher-facing Q&A bot + admin digest bot. Begin build during fall pilot. Deploy to a subset of schools by winter 2026. Depends on H1 Airtable infrastructure being live.
Horizon 3 — Long-term
Platform architecture decision
Where does curriculum live? A360 as LMS, custom microsite, or Drive + deep instrumentation. This decision unlocks true passive tracking. Requires Steve's input and a team decision.

Chipp Bot — positioning and open questions

Why Chipp Bot is Horizon 2 (mid-term), not Horizon 3 (long-term)

The original brief classified the bot as Horizon 2/long-term. Based on review of the Chipp Bot PRD v1, the teacher Q&A bot (Phase 1 — no Drive scraping, no student data, no cross-site access) is a 2–4 week build on Chipp.ai. It is not a multi-month engineering project. The barriers are: (1) answering the technical verification questions below, and (2) building the MI curriculum knowledge base that powers it. Both are doable during the fall pilot. Repositioning to mid-term is correct.

What the teacher bot does in Phase 1

  • Answers curriculum and facilitation questions grounded in MI knowledge base (LPs, module wrappers, best practices)
  • Generates customized checklists adapted to the teacher's specific partner and pacing
  • Walks teacher through pacing mapper conversationally
  • Points teachers to the right resources without Ivan or Farah being in the loop
  • Does NOT: access student data, read Drive folders, or cross-reference other schools

What the admin bot does in Phase 1

  • Answers Ivan's questions about implementation health in plain language: "How is Roosevelt High doing?"
  • Delivers daily digest from Airtable data without Ivan opening Airtable
  • Surfaces flags from pulse data and tier changes
  • Does NOT (in Phase 1): read Drive files, assess student work quality, or perform Drive monitoring

What the bot doesn't solve (be honest with the team)

The Chipp Bot sits in the weakest-signal tier of the platform options. Drive-based monitoring gives you document open/edit events — not time-on-task, reading depth, or whether a teacher engaged with content vs. skimmed it. The bot makes that limited signal more conversationally accessible; it doesn't increase underlying signal quality. The passive tracking problem (knowing when curriculum is opened and engaged with) is only truly solved by moving curriculum into a trackable platform — A360 or a custom microsite. That's Horizon 3. The bot is a meaningful step forward, not a complete solution.

Open technical questions — answer these before building

Can Chipp.ai natively perform continuous/scheduled Drive monitoring, or must that live in Apps Script/Zapier that Chipp then reads from? This is the most important question — it determines the entire infrastructure architecture.
Can Chipp.ai deliver a proactive scheduled digest (without Ivan asking), or does something else need to assemble and push it?
Does Chipp.ai support role-based access control within one bot instance, or is the two-bot architecture required at the platform level?
What LLM does Chipp.ai run on, and does it reliably follow MI format rules (8th-grade reading level, no jargon)?
Does Chipp.ai support a native SMS channel? If yes, the texting tool decision is resolved — use the bot.
What are Chipp.ai's contractual data-retention terms with its LLM provider? Required for Cambiar Legal submission before any student data flows through the system.
Who owns the Chipp.ai configuration and Airtable schema build — Ivan solo, Steve, or outside dev support?

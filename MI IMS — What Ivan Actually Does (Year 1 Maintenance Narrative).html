What Ivan Actually Does — Year 1 IMS

A plain-language account of the balance between automation maintenance and manual labor across a full fall cycle. Written for honest team expectations-setting.

The Short Version

Once the H1 system is built and running, Ivan's job shifts from doing implementation management to watching the system do it and intervening where the system flags a need. The system handles routine — Ivan handles judgment. The hard part is the 3-4 week build before fall launch. After that, steady-state maintenance is lighter than what's currently happening manually.

Phase 1 — Before Launch (Weeks 1-4): The Build

This is the highest-effort period. Ivan isn't doing implementation management yet — he's building the system that will do it. Estimated 20-25 hours total across 4 weeks, sequenced so content exists before automation tries to deliver it.

Week 1: Airtable foundation

  • Add 22 new fields to Program Implementation table (~3 hrs)
  • Create Weekly Pulse Log table with linking to parent records (~2 hrs)
  • Build pulse form in Google Forms — mobile-optimized, conditional logic for module deliverable question (~2 hrs)
  • Build Zap 1 (welcome email) and Zap 4 (pulse form to Airtable) (~5 hrs total — Zap 4 is complex, budget extra debugging time)

Week 2: Training automation + pacing mapper

  • Build pacing mapper Google Sheet (formula logic for session calendar + risk flags) (~3 hrs)
  • Set up Calendly for Part 2 PD windows (3-4 event types) (~1 hr)
  • Build Zap 2 (Reach 360 webhook to Part 2 window email) with Steve's help (~2 hrs)
  • Build Zap 3 (Calendly to Airtable) and launch gate 7-item checklist + all-green alert (~2 hrs)

Week 3: JIT content + module transition automation

  • Record M1 Loom kit (2-3 min): client kickoff coaching moves (~1 hr including prep)
  • Record M2 Loom kit (2-3 min): interview quality coaching (~1 hr)
  • Record M3 Loom kit (2-3 min): synthesis leap coaching — hardest to get right (~2 hrs, may need second take)
  • Build Zap 5 (missed pulse to Saturday reminder) and Zap 6 (module transition to JIT kits) (~5 hrs including testing)
  • Build client presentation rubric + 2-3 exemplar summaries (~3 hrs — content task)

Week 4: Showcase, closeout, digest, interface

  • Build Zap 7 (T-21 showcase trigger), Zap 8 (post-showcase surveys), Zap 9 (Monday digest) (~7 hrs — digest formatting is the hardest part)
  • Build Airtable Interface (Implementation Control Center) (~3 hrs)
  • Test entire system with 2 synthetic records before any school goes live (~2 hrs — do not skip this)
The build is front-loaded. If Ivan does not finish weeks 1-2 before the first pilot school's launch date, the automation has nothing to run on. Pacing mapper and Airtable structure are blocking dependencies — everything downstream depends on them first.

System Maintenance: What Can Break and How to Fix It

Zap failures (most common issue)

  • Prevention: never rename Airtable fields or edit form question text without also updating the corresponding Zap
  • Detection: Monday digest doesn't arrive — check Zapier error log immediately
  • Fix: Zapier error screen shows exactly which step failed and why. Most fixes take under 15 minutes.
  • If Zap 4 (pulse form to Airtable) breaks: collect pulse responses manually from Google Forms until fixed. Don't let it sit more than 24 hours.

Airtable formula errors

  • Detection: tier field shows blank or wrong value; survey completion rate shows 0% despite submissions
  • Fix: check that Weekly Pulse Log records are correctly linked to parent implementation records. Re-link manually if not.
  • Prevention: test with 2 synthetic records before any school goes live.

Pulse form compliance dropping

  • One school misses two consecutive weeks: Ivan direct call — this is a Red flag by definition
  • Multiple schools miss the same week: the form itself may have broken (link expired, form closed). Check Google Forms admin.
  • Compliance below 70% after 4 weeks: adjust incentive structure before spring — this is a pilot retrospective agenda item

Loom kit tracking

  • Setup once: use Mailchimp or Gmail link tracking to track JIT kit email open and click rates before pilot launches
  • Check monthly: if a kit has under 50% open rate, send it as a personal text with the Loom link instead of batch email
  • When curriculum changes significantly: re-record the corresponding kit (~1 hr)
Before vs. After: The Honest Comparison
With H1 IMS (fall pilot)

Ivan opens a digest email Monday. 4 Green schools — no action. 1 Yellow school entering M3 with a missed pulse — sends a personal text, follows up Wednesday. JIT kits already went out automatically. Client check-ins already went out automatically. Monday triage: 30 minutes. Remaining week: under 1 hour reactive.

Without IMS (current state)

Ivan has no signal unless a teacher reaches out. He doesn't know School C entered M3 last week or that School A hasn't submitted anything in 10 days. Farah is building manual pacing maps. JIT kits don't exist. The M3 moment passes without support. By the time it's visible at the showcase, it's too late to fix.

The system's core value: it doesn't make Ivan's job easier when things go wrong. It makes things go wrong less often — because drift is caught at week 2, not week 8. The Monday digest is 30 minutes, not 3 hours, because you're triaging flags rather than reconstructing status from scratch.
